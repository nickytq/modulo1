Learn Braille and promote accessibility

The Braille system allows people with visual impairments to read and write using raised dots. On this page, you'll learn how it works and how to use it.

Thanks to Braille, many people with visual impairments can access books, study at school, write notes, and communicate more independently.

What is the Braille system?

The Braille system is a tactile reading and writing system used by people with visual impairments. It was created by Louis Braille in the 19th century and is based on combinations of six raised dots that form letters, numbers, and symbols.
